Step 2

Host your website with GitHub Pages

Once you've built your website with Web IDE, get it live online.

Step 1

Create GitHub Account & Repo

Sign up for a free GitHub account. Once logged in, create a new public repository.

Step 2

Upload Your Project Files

In your new GitHub repository, click "Add file" then "Upload files". Drag and drop your project's `index.html` from the zip exported by Web IDE.

Step 3

Commit Your Changes

After uploading all your file, scroll down to the bottom of the page and click the green "Commit changes" button. This saves your files to the repository.

Step 4

Go to GitHub Pages Settings

Navigate to your repository's main page. Click on the "Settings" tab, then select "Pages" from the left-hand sidebar.

Step 5

Configure Deployment Source

Under "Build and deployment" for "Source", choose "Deploy from a branch". Set the branch to "main" (or "master") and the folder to "/(root)". Click "Save".

Step 6

Wait for Your Site to Go Live

GitHub Pages will now build and deploy your site. This usually takes around 5 minutes. Refresh the "Pages" settings, and your live website URL will appear at the top!
